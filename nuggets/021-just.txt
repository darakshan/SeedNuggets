#title The Word "Just" Is a Confession
#subtitle When someone says a process is *just* doing something, they've described the output and mistaken it for an explanation.
#status prelim
#date 2026-03-11
#tags language, rhetoric, cognition, reductionism, dismissal
#related 006

#surface
The word "just" does a specific rhetorical job — it closes down curiosity by making something sound fully accounted for. It's just a rock rolling downhill. It's just neurons firing. It's just predicting words. But "just" always points at an output or a mechanism described at one level of abstraction. It never reaches the process. Every time you hear it, something interesting is being dismissed. The word is almost always a flag that the speaker has stopped looking too soon.
